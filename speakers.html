Keynote Speakers

Esteemed Space Physiology and Biochemistry Expert: Prof. Bill Kurk

Prof. Bill Kurk (he/him) is a highly esteemed Lecturer of Physiology and Biochemistry at the University of Aberdeenshire. With a distinguished academic career, he brings profound knowledge and expertise in the field of space physiology and biochemistry. His groundbreaking research delves into the effects of space travel on the human body, exploring how zero-gravity environments influence biological functions and processes. Prof. Kurk is known for his innovative approach to mitigating health risks associated with prolonged space missions. His contributions have significantly advanced the understanding of human adaptability in space and have paved the way for safer and more efficient space exploration. Throughout his career, Prof. Kurk has received numerous accolades and honors for his impactful work. These include the *Space Medicine Achievement Award* for his contributions to the field of space medicine, and the *Galileo Science Medal* for his innovative research in biochemistry. He has also been recognized with the *International Space Research Excellence Award* for his exceptional dedication to advancing human knowledge in space exploration. His engaging teaching style and commitment to nurturing the next generation of scientists have made him a highly respected figure in the scientific community. Prof. Kurk's passion for pushing the boundaries of human knowledge in space exploration continues to inspire both his students and peers.
